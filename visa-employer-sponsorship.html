Employer Sponsorship Visas

Employer sponsorship is one of the most common pathways for skilled workers to live and work in Australia. It allows Australian businesses to sponsor overseas workers when they cannot find suitably qualified Australians to fill positions.

The Australian Government offers employer-sponsored visa programs to address genuine skill shortages in the labour market. These programs require employers to meet strict obligations, including paying market salary rates and ensuring genuine need for the position. Granger Australia assists both employers seeking to sponsor workers and employees navigating the sponsorship process.

Subclass 482 — Temporary Skill Shortage (TSS) Visa

The Subclass 482 visa allows employers to sponsor skilled workers from overseas to fill positions where there is a genuine shortage of qualified Australian workers. This is a temporary visa, but depending on the stream, it may provide a pathway to permanent residence.

Short-Term Stream

  • For occupations listed on the Short-Term Skilled Occupation List (STSOL)
  • Visa granted for up to two years, with the possibility of one renewal onshore
  • Does not generally provide a direct pathway to permanent residence
  • Applicants must have at least two years of relevant work experience
  • English language requirements apply (generally IELTS 5.0 overall, with no band less than 4.5, or equivalent)

Medium-Term Stream

  • For occupations listed on the Medium and Long-Term Strategic Skills List (MLTSSL) or Regional Occupation List (ROL)
  • Visa granted for up to four years
  • Provides a pathway to permanent residence through the Subclass 186 Temporary Residence Transition stream after three years
  • Applicants must have at least two years of relevant work experience
  • Higher English language requirements apply (generally IELTS 5.0 overall, with no band less than 5.0, or equivalent)

Key Requirements for the 482 Visa

  • Approved sponsor: The employer must be an approved Standard Business Sponsor, or apply to become one
  • Nomination: The employer must nominate the position and demonstrate a genuine need for the role
  • Labour market testing: Employers must provide evidence they have tested the Australian labour market and were unable to find a suitable Australian worker
  • Skills assessment: A relevant skills assessment may be required depending on the occupation
  • Qualifications and experience: The applicant must hold qualifications and experience relevant to the nominated occupation
  • Health and character: Standard health and character requirements apply
  • Salary: The employer must pay at least the annual market salary rate and meet the Temporary Skilled Migration Income Threshold (TSMIT)

Subclass 186 — Employer Nomination Scheme (ENS)

The Subclass 186 visa is a permanent residence visa for skilled workers who are nominated by their employer. It offers three streams, each with different eligibility criteria.

Temporary Residence Transition (TRT) Stream

For holders of a Subclass 482 (Medium-Term stream) visa who have worked for their sponsoring employer for at least three years.

  • Must have held a Subclass 482 visa (Medium-Term stream) or a previous Subclass 457 visa
  • Must have worked for the nominating employer for at least three years in the nominated or a closely related occupation
  • The employer must demonstrate the position is genuine and ongoing
  • Applicants must generally be under 45 years of age at the time of application (exemptions may apply)
  • English language competency must be demonstrated

Direct Entry Stream

For applicants who have not previously held a 482 or 457 visa, or who do not meet the TRT stream requirements.

  • Occupation must be on the relevant skilled occupation list
  • A positive skills assessment from the relevant assessing authority is required
  • Applicants must generally be under 45 years of age (exemptions may apply)
  • Competent English is required (generally IELTS 6.0 in each band, or equivalent)
  • At least three years of relevant work experience (unless an exemption applies)
  • The employer must be an approved sponsor and nominate the position

Labour Agreement Stream

For workers sponsored by an employer through a labour agreement with the Australian Government.

  • The employer must have a labour agreement in effect with the Commonwealth
  • The agreement must specify that the employer can sponsor workers for permanent residence
  • Requirements vary depending on the terms of the specific labour agreement
  • This stream is typically used by industries with unique or specialised labour needs

General Requirements and Employer Obligations

For Employers

  • Standard Business Sponsorship: Employers must apply and be approved as a Standard Business Sponsor before nominating a worker
  • Genuine position: The nominated position must be genuine, full-time, and reflect actual business needs
  • Market salary rate: Employers must pay the sponsored worker at least the annual market salary rate for the occupation in the relevant location
  • Training obligations: Employers must contribute to the Skilling Australians Fund (SAF) levy
  • Record keeping: Employers must keep records and cooperate with monitoring by the Department of Home Affairs
  • Non-discriminatory terms: Employment conditions must be no less favourable than those provided to an equivalent Australian worker

For Applicants

  • English language: Applicants must demonstrate English proficiency at the level required for their visa stream (IELTS, PTE, TOEFL iBT, OET, or Cambridge are accepted)
  • Skills and qualifications: Must hold qualifications and experience relevant to the nominated occupation
  • Health: Must meet health requirements, including medical examinations
  • Character: Must meet character requirements, including police clearances
  • Age: For permanent visas, applicants must generally be under 45 years of age at the time of application, though exemptions exist in certain circumstances

Salary Thresholds

The Australian Government sets minimum salary thresholds for employer-sponsored visas, including the Temporary Skilled Migration Income Threshold (TSMIT). These thresholds are reviewed and updated periodically. Please contact Granger Australia or visit the Department of Home Affairs website for the current applicable thresholds.

Processing Times

Processing times for employer sponsorship visas vary depending on the visa subclass, stream, and individual circumstances. The Department of Home Affairs publishes indicative processing times on their website, which are updated regularly. Granger Australia recommends lodging applications as early as possible and ensuring all supporting documentation is complete to avoid delays.
