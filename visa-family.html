Family Visas

  1. Home

Australia's family visa programme enables Australian citizens, permanent residents and eligible New Zealand citizens to sponsor close family members to live in Australia permanently. Granger Australia provides compassionate and expert guidance through the family reunion process, ensuring your application is thoroughly prepared and properly lodged.

Child Visa - Subclass 802

The Subclass 802 visa allows a dependent child of an Australian citizen, permanent resident or eligible New Zealand citizen to remain in Australia permanently. The child must be in Australia when the application is lodged and when the visa is granted.

Key Requirements

  • The child must be sponsored by an eligible parent who is an Australian citizen, permanent resident or eligible New Zealand citizen
  • The child must be under 18 years of age, or if aged 18-25, must be a full-time student financially dependent on the sponsoring parent
  • A dependent child over 18 may also be eligible if they have a disability and are dependent on the parent
  • The child must be in Australia at the time of application and at the time of visa grant
  • Must meet health and character requirements

Parent Visas

Parent visas allow the parents of settled Australian citizens, permanent residents or eligible New Zealand citizens to live in Australia. There are several parent visa options, each with different costs, processing times and requirements.

Aged Parent Visa - Subclass 804

For parents who are old enough to receive the Australian age pension. This is a permanent visa with a lower visa application charge but significantly longer processing times.

  • The parent must be old enough to receive the Australian age pension
  • Must pass the balance of family test (at least half of their children live in Australia, or more children live in Australia than any other single country)
  • Must be sponsored by their child or the child's spouse/de facto partner
  • The sponsor must have been a settled Australian resident for at least two years
  • An assurance of support may be required

Contributory Parent Visa - Subclass 143

A permanent visa for parents who are willing to pay a higher visa application charge in exchange for faster processing. This is the most popular parent visa category.

  • Must pass the balance of family test
  • Must be sponsored by their child or the child's spouse/de facto partner
  • Requires payment of a second instalment of the visa application charge (a significant contribution to the cost of health care in Australia)
  • An assurance of support is required
  • Generally processed faster than the standard Aged Parent visa

Contributory Parent (Temporary) Visa - Subclass 173

A temporary visa that allows parents to live in Australia for up to two years while waiting for the permanent Contributory Parent (Subclass 143) visa. This option allows the visa application charge to be paid in two stages.

  • Same balance of family test and sponsorship requirements as the Subclass 143
  • Allows the parent to live and work in Australia temporarily
  • Must apply for the Subclass 143 within two years of the 173 grant to obtain permanent residency
  • Useful for families who wish to spread the cost of the visa over a longer period

The Balance of Family Test

To be eligible for a parent visa, the applicant must pass the balance of family test. This means that at least half of their children must live permanently in Australia, or more of their children live in Australia than in any other single country. This test considers all of the parent's children, including stepchildren and adopted children.

Remaining Relative Visa - Subclass 835

The Subclass 835 visa is for applicants whose only near relatives are in Australia. This visa allows the applicant to remain in Australia permanently.

Key Requirements

  • The applicant's only near relatives (parent, brother, sister, or adult child) must be Australian citizens, permanent residents or eligible New Zealand citizens living in Australia
  • The applicant must not have any near relatives living outside Australia (other than the applicant's partner or dependent children)
  • Must be sponsored by a near relative in Australia
  • Must be in Australia at the time of application and at the time of visa grant
  • Must meet health and character requirements

Carer Visa - Subclass 836

The Subclass 836 visa is for applicants who are willing and able to provide care to a relative in Australia who has a medical condition. The applicant must be in Australia when the application is lodged.

Key Requirements

  • Must provide ongoing assistance to an Australian relative who has a long-term medical condition
  • The relative must need ongoing assistance from the applicant, and this assistance must not be reasonably available from Australian welfare, health or community services, or from any other relative in Australia
  • A medical opinion from a government-appointed medical officer is required to support the claim
  • Must be sponsored by the relative they will be caring for, or by another eligible person
  • Must be in Australia at the time of application and at the time of visa grant

Sponsor Requirements

All family visa applications require an eligible sponsor. General sponsor requirements include:

  • The sponsor must be an Australian citizen, permanent resident or eligible New Zealand citizen
  • The sponsor must usually be at least 18 years of age
  • For parent visas, the sponsoring child must generally have been a settled Australian resident for at least two years
  • The sponsor may need to provide an assurance of support (a financial guarantee to the Australian Government)
  • The sponsor must meet character requirements
Processing Times

Processing times for family visas vary significantly depending on the visa subclass and the current queue. Some family visa categories, particularly non-contributory parent visas, have very long processing times. For current processing times, visit the Department of Home Affairs website.
