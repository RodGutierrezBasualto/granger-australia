Australia's Business Innovation and Investment Programme is designed to attract experienced business people, entrepreneurs and investors who can contribute to the Australian economy. Granger Australia provides expert guidance on business and investor visa pathways, from initial state nomination through to permanent residency.

Business Innovation and Investment (Provisional) Visa - Subclass 188

The Subclass 188 visa is a provisional visa for business owners, investors and entrepreneurs who want to establish or manage a business in Australia, or make a designated investment. It is granted for a period of up to five years and provides a pathway to permanent residency through the Subclass 888 visa.

Streams

Business Innovation Stream

For applicants with a successful business career who want to own and manage a new or existing business in Australia.

  • Must have an overall successful business career
  • Business and personal assets of at least AUD 1.25 million
  • Annual business turnover of at least AUD 750,000 in at least two of the four fiscal years preceding the application
  • Must be under 55 years of age (unless the nominating state or territory certifies exceptional economic benefit)
  • Score at least 65 points on the business innovation and investment points test
  • Must be nominated by an Australian state or territory government
Investor Stream

For applicants who are willing to invest at least AUD 2.5 million in an Australian state or territory and maintain business and investment activity in Australia.

  • At least three years of experience managing qualifying investments or a business
  • Business and personal net assets of at least AUD 2.5 million
  • Must make and maintain a designated investment of at least AUD 2.5 million in the nominating state or territory
  • Score at least 65 points on the business innovation and investment points test
  • Must be nominated by an Australian state or territory government
Significant Investor Stream

For applicants willing to invest at least AUD 5 million in complying significant investments in Australia.

  • Must make and maintain a complying significant investment of at least AUD 5 million
  • Investment must be held in a complying investment framework: at least AUD 500,000 in eligible Australian venture capital or growth private equity funds; at least AUD 1.5 million in approved managed funds investing in emerging companies listed on the ASX; and a balancing investment of at least AUD 3 million in managed funds investing in a range of eligible assets
  • No age limit or points test requirement
  • Must be nominated by an Australian state or territory government or Austrade
Entrepreneur Stream

For applicants who have a funding agreement of at least AUD 200,000 from a specified entity to undertake a complying entrepreneur activity in Australia.

  • Must have a funding agreement from an eligible third party (Commonwealth agency, state/territory government, publicly funded research organisation, or registered investor)
  • The entrepreneur activity must relate to an innovative product or service, and must not be in residential real estate, labour hire, tobacco, or other excluded activities
  • Must be under 55 years of age (unless exempted by the nominating body)
  • Must have competent English (IELTS 6.0 or equivalent in each band)
  • Must be nominated by an Australian state or territory government

Business Innovation and Investment (Permanent) Visa - Subclass 888

The Subclass 888 visa is the permanent visa for holders of the Subclass 188 provisional visa who have met the requirements of their respective stream during the provisional period.

Pathway from 188 to 888

Business Innovation Stream (888)
  • Must have held the 188 visa for at least two years
  • Must have ownership interest in an established business in Australia
  • Business must have an annual turnover of at least AUD 300,000
  • Meet at least two of three additional criteria: business assets, number of employees, business turnover thresholds
Investor Stream (888)
  • Must have held the designated investment in the nominating state/territory for at least four years
  • Must have maintained the investment at the required level
  • Must have a genuine and realistic commitment to continuing business or investment activity in Australia
Significant Investor Stream (888)
  • Must have held the complying significant investment for at least four years
  • Must have maintained the investment within the complying framework throughout
  • Must demonstrate a genuine and realistic commitment to continuing business or investment activity in Australia
Entrepreneur Stream (888)
  • Must have held a Subclass 188 Entrepreneur stream visa
  • Must demonstrate success in the entrepreneur activity
  • Activity must have operated for at least two of the four years on the 188 visa

State and Territory Nomination

All business and investor visa applicants must obtain a nomination from an Australian state or territory government (or Austrade for the Significant Investor stream). Each state and territory has its own criteria and priority sectors. Granger Australia can advise on the most suitable nominating body based on your business plans, investment preferences and intended location.

Processing Times

Processing times for business and investor visas vary depending on the stream, complexity of the application, and the Department of Home Affairs' workload. For current processing times, visit the Department of Home Affairs website.
