Australia is one of the world's most popular study destinations, offering high-quality education across all sectors. The Student Visa (Subclass 500) allows international students to study full-time at an Australian educational institution. Granger Australia can help you navigate the application process and understand your obligations as a student visa holder.

Student Visa - Subclass 500

The Subclass 500 visa covers all education sectors in Australia. Regardless of the type of study you are undertaking, you will apply for the same visa subclass. The visa allows you to study full-time in Australia for the duration of your course.

Education Sectors

Higher Education

Bachelor degrees, master's degrees and doctoral programmes at Australian universities. Australia has world-ranked universities offering a wide range of disciplines.

Vocational Education (VET)

Certificate and diploma courses at registered training organisations (RTOs) and TAFE institutes. VET courses provide practical, industry-focused training.

English Language (ELICOS)

English Language Intensive Courses for Overseas Students. These courses improve your English for further study, work or everyday communication in Australia.

Schools

Primary and secondary school studies at Australian government or private schools. Students under 18 must have appropriate welfare arrangements in place.

Postgraduate Research

Master's by research and doctoral (PhD) programmes at Australian universities. Research students may have access to additional work rights and benefits.

Non-Award & Exchange

Foundation studies, study abroad and exchange programmes. These can serve as pathways to further study or provide international academic experience.

General Requirements

Confirmation of Enrolment (CoE)

You must have a valid Confirmation of Enrolment (CoE) from an Australian education provider registered on the Commonwealth Register of Institutions and Courses for Overseas Students (CRICOS). The CoE confirms your enrolment in a full-time course.

Genuine Student Requirement

You must demonstrate that you are a genuine student who intends to study in Australia. The Department of Home Affairs will assess your circumstances, including your study history, the relevance of your chosen course to your future plans, your ties to your home country, and the conditions in your home country. You may be asked to provide a written statement or attend an interview.

English Language Proficiency

You must demonstrate English language proficiency. Accepted tests include IELTS, TOEFL iBT, PTE Academic, Cambridge C1 Advanced, and others. The required score depends on your course and education provider.

Some applicants may be exempt from English testing if they are citizens of certain countries, have completed sufficient study in English, or meet other specified criteria.

Financial Capacity

You must demonstrate that you have access to sufficient funds to cover your tuition fees, living costs and travel to and from Australia. Evidence may include:

  • Bank statements showing sufficient savings
  • Evidence of a scholarship or financial support from a government agency
  • Evidence of income from a parent, partner or spouse
  • A loan from a financial institution

Overseas Student Health Cover (OSHC)

Student visa holders must maintain adequate Overseas Student Health Cover (OSHC) for the duration of their stay in Australia. OSHC covers hospital and medical treatment. You must arrange OSHC before your visa is granted, and it must cover you from the date you arrive in Australia until the end of your visa.

Other Requirements

  • Meet health and character requirements
  • Not owe any outstanding debts to the Australian Government
  • Not have had a visa cancelled or an application refused previously (or declare any such history)
  • Have appropriate welfare arrangements if under 18 years of age

Work Rights

Student visa holders are generally permitted to work in Australia, subject to the following conditions:

  • During study sessions: You may work up to 48 hours per fortnight (a fortnight is a two-week period beginning on a Monday)
  • During scheduled course breaks: There is no limit on the hours you can work during official scheduled breaks in your course
  • Work as part of your course: Any work that is a registered component of your course does not count towards the 48-hour limit
  • Postgraduate research students: Once your course has commenced, there is no limit on hours of work

Note: You cannot begin work until your course has commenced. Work conditions may change, so always check the conditions attached to your specific visa grant.

Pathway After Study: Temporary Graduate Visa (Subclass 485)

After completing your studies in Australia, you may be eligible for the Temporary Graduate Visa (Subclass 485), which allows you to live and work in Australia temporarily after graduating. There are two main streams:

Graduate Work Stream

For graduates with skills and qualifications relevant to an occupation on the Medium and Long-term Strategic Skills List (MLTSSL). This stream grants a visa for 18 months.

Post-Study Work Stream

For graduates who hold a higher education degree (bachelor's, master's or doctoral) from an Australian institution. Visa duration is two to four years depending on the qualification level, with extended durations available for regional graduates.

Processing Times

Processing times for student visas vary depending on the applicant's nationality, the education sector and the completeness of the application. For current processing times, visit the Department of Home Affairs website.
