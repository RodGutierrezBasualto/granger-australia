Skilled Migration to Australia

Australia's skilled migration programme is designed to attract qualified professionals whose skills are in demand across the Australian economy. If you hold qualifications and work experience in a listed skilled occupation, you may be eligible for a permanent or provisional visa that allows you to live and work in Australia.

The General Skilled Migration (GSM) programme uses a points-based system to rank candidates. You must submit an Expression of Interest (EOI) through the Department of Home Affairs' SkillSelect system and receive an invitation to apply. Points are awarded for factors such as age, English language ability, work experience, educational qualifications, and other criteria.

Granger Australia has been helping skilled migrants navigate this process since 1999. Below you will find an overview of the three main skilled worker visa subclasses currently available.

Subclass 189 — Skilled Independent Visa

Permanent Residence | No Sponsor Required

The Subclass 189 visa is a permanent residence visa for skilled workers who are not sponsored by an employer, a state or territory government, or a family member. It is the most sought-after skilled visa because it allows you to live and work anywhere in Australia with no obligations to a particular employer or region.

Key Features
  • Grants permanent residence from the date of visa grant
  • No requirement for employer, state, or territory sponsorship
  • Freedom to live and work anywhere in Australia
  • Access to Medicare (Australia's public healthcare system)
  • Eligibility to apply for Australian citizenship (subject to meeting citizenship requirements)
  • Ability to sponsor eligible relatives for permanent residence
  • Travel to and from Australia for five years from the date of grant
Eligibility Requirements
  • Your occupation must be on the relevant Skilled Occupation List
  • You must obtain a positive skills assessment from the relevant assessing authority for your occupation
  • You must score a minimum of 65 points on the points test (though competitive scores are typically higher)
  • You must meet minimum English language requirements (generally Competent English or higher)
  • You must be under 45 years of age at the time of invitation
  • You must meet health and character requirements
Application Process
  • Step 1: Obtain a skills assessment from the relevant assessing authority
  • Step 2: Sit an English language test (IELTS, PTE Academic, TOEFL iBT, OET, or Cambridge C1 Advanced)
  • Step 3: Submit an Expression of Interest (EOI) through SkillSelect
  • Step 4: Receive an invitation to apply (invitations are issued in regular rounds based on points ranking)
  • Step 5: Lodge the visa application within 60 days of invitation
  • Step 6: Provide supporting documents, undergo health examinations and police checks

Processing times: Processing times vary and are subject to change. Please refer to the Department of Home Affairs website for current estimated processing times.

Subclass 190 — Skilled Nominated Visa

Permanent Residence | State or Territory Nomination

The Subclass 190 visa is a permanent residence visa for skilled workers who are nominated by an Australian state or territory government. Nomination provides an additional 5 points towards the points test, making this an excellent pathway for applicants who may not have a high enough score for the Subclass 189.

Key Features
  • Grants permanent residence from the date of visa grant
  • State or territory nomination adds 5 points to your points test score
  • Access to Medicare and the same benefits as other permanent residents
  • Eligibility to apply for Australian citizenship
  • Travel to and from Australia for five years from the date of grant
Eligibility Requirements
  • Your occupation must be on the relevant Skilled Occupation List for the nominating state or territory
  • You must obtain a positive skills assessment
  • You must score a minimum of 65 points (including the 5 nomination points)
  • You must receive a nomination from an Australian state or territory government
  • You must meet minimum English language requirements
  • You must be under 45 years of age at the time of invitation
  • You must meet health and character requirements
State or Territory Nomination

Each state and territory has its own nomination criteria, occupation lists, and application processes. Generally, you will need to demonstrate a genuine intention to live and work in the nominating state or territory. Key conditions include:

  • You must commit to living in the nominating state or territory for at least two years after the visa is granted
  • Some states require you to have work experience, a job offer, or other connections to the state
  • Nomination criteria and available occupations change regularly, so it is important to check the current requirements
Application Process
  • Step 1: Obtain a skills assessment and English language test results
  • Step 2: Submit an EOI through SkillSelect, selecting the state or territory you wish to be nominated by
  • Step 3: Apply for state or territory nomination (this is a separate application to the relevant state authority)
  • Step 4: If nominated, you will receive an invitation to apply for the visa through SkillSelect
  • Step 5: Lodge the visa application within 60 days of invitation
  • Step 6: Provide supporting documents, health examinations, and police checks

Processing times: Processing times vary depending on individual circumstances. Visit the Department of Home Affairs website for the latest processing time estimates.

Subclass 491 — Skilled Work Regional (Provisional) Visa

Provisional Visa (5 Years) | Regional Area | Pathway to Permanent Residence

The Subclass 491 visa is a provisional visa for skilled workers who want to live and work in regional Australia. It is valid for five years and provides a pathway to permanent residence through the Subclass 191 (Permanent Residence — Skilled Regional) visa. This visa is an attractive option for applicants who are open to regional living, as the nomination provides a significant boost of 15 points.

Key Features
  • Provisional visa valid for five years
  • State, territory, or eligible family member nomination adds 15 points to your points test score
  • Must live, work, and study in a designated regional area of Australia
  • Access to Medicare during the visa validity period
  • Pathway to permanent residence via the Subclass 191 visa after living and working in a regional area for at least three years
Eligibility Requirements
  • Your occupation must be on the relevant Skilled Occupation List
  • You must obtain a positive skills assessment
  • You must score a minimum of 65 points (including the 15 nomination points)
  • You must be nominated by an Australian state or territory government, or sponsored by an eligible family member living in a designated regional area
  • You must meet minimum English language requirements
  • You must be under 45 years of age at the time of invitation
  • You must meet health and character requirements
What is a Designated Regional Area?

Designated regional areas include all of Australia except Sydney, Melbourne, and Brisbane. This means cities such as Adelaide, Perth, Hobart, Canberra, Darwin, the Gold Coast, and all rural and remote areas are classified as regional for the purposes of this visa.

Pathway to Permanent Residence

After holding the Subclass 491 visa, you may be eligible to apply for the Subclass 191 (Permanent Residence — Skilled Regional) visa, provided you:

  • Have held a Subclass 491 visa for at least three years
  • Have lived and worked in a designated regional area during that time
  • Have met the minimum income threshold as set by the Department of Home Affairs
  • Have complied with all visa conditions

Processing times: Processing times are subject to change. Please check the Department of Home Affairs website for the most up-to-date information.

General Requirements for Skilled Visas

Skills Assessment

All skilled visa applicants must obtain a positive skills assessment from the assessing authority designated for their nominated occupation. Each occupation has a specific assessing body (for example, Engineers Australia for engineers, CPA Australia or CAANZ for accountants, ACS for ICT professionals). The skills assessment confirms that your qualifications and experience meet the Australian standard for your occupation.

English Language Proficiency

You must demonstrate English language ability through one of the following accepted tests:

  • IELTS (International English Language Testing System)
  • PTE Academic (Pearson Test of English)
  • TOEFL iBT (Test of English as a Foreign Language)
  • OET (Occupational English Test)
  • Cambridge C1 Advanced

Higher English scores earn more points on the points test. Most skilled visas require at least Competent English, but Superior or Proficient English will significantly improve your score.

The Points Test

You must score a minimum of 65 points to be eligible. Points are awarded for the following factors:

  • Age: Maximum points for applicants aged 25 to 32
  • English language ability: Higher proficiency scores earn more points
  • Skilled employment experience: Points for overseas and Australian work experience in your nominated occupation
  • Educational qualifications: Points for doctorate, bachelor's degree, diploma, or trade qualifications
  • Australian study requirement: Additional points if you completed qualifying study in Australia
  • Specialist education qualification: Points for a Masters by research or Doctorate from an Australian institution in a STEM field
  • Accredited community language: Points for NAATI accreditation
  • Study in regional Australia: Additional points for study undertaken in a regional area
  • Partner skills: Points if your partner also meets skills and English requirements
  • Professional Year: Points for completing a Professional Year programme in Australia
  • State/territory nomination: 5 points (Subclass 190) or 15 points (Subclass 491)

While 65 points is the minimum, the actual invitation score varies by occupation and invitation round. Many occupations require significantly higher scores. Granger Australia can advise you on your realistic prospects based on current trends.

Expression of Interest (EOI) via SkillSelect

All skilled visa applicants must submit an Expression of Interest (EOI) through the Department of Home Affairs' SkillSelect portal. Your EOI remains active for two years. Invitations to apply are issued in regular rounds, with the highest-scoring candidates invited first. It is essential that all information in your EOI is accurate, as the Department may audit claims at any stage.
