Regional Sponsorship

  1. Home

Australia's regional migration programme offers skilled workers an excellent pathway to permanent residency while supporting the growth of regional communities. Granger Australia can guide you through the regional visa options, helping you find the right pathway based on your skills, qualifications and circumstances.

Skilled Employer Sponsored Regional (Provisional) Visa - Subclass 494

The Subclass 494 visa is a provisional visa for skilled workers sponsored by an employer in a designated regional area of Australia. This visa allows holders to live and work in regional Australia for up to five years, with a pathway to permanent residency through the Subclass 191 visa.

Key Streams

Employer Sponsored Stream
  • The applicant must be nominated by an approved regional employer
  • The position must be on the relevant skilled occupation list
  • A skills assessment may be required depending on the occupation
Labour Agreement Stream
  • For workers sponsored through a labour agreement between the employer and the Australian Government
  • Addresses specific labour shortages in regional areas
  • Conditions are set out in the individual labour agreement

General Requirements for Subclass 494

  • Be under 45 years of age at the time of application (unless exempt)
  • Have at least competent English (IELTS 6.0 in each band or equivalent)
  • Hold relevant qualifications and/or skills assessment for the nominated occupation
  • Have at least three years of relevant work experience
  • Be nominated by an approved sponsor in a designated regional area
  • Meet health and character requirements
What is a Designated Regional Area?

Designated regional areas include all of Australia except Sydney, Melbourne, and Brisbane. This means cities such as Perth, Adelaide, the Gold Coast, Canberra, Newcastle, Wollongong and all rural and remote areas are classified as regional for the purposes of this visa.

Permanent Residence (Skilled Regional) Visa - Subclass 191

The Subclass 191 visa provides a pathway from provisional regional visas (such as the 494 and 491) to permanent residency. It is designed for holders of qualifying provisional regional visas who have lived and worked in a designated regional area of Australia.

Key Requirements for Subclass 191

  • Hold or have held a qualifying provisional visa (Subclass 491 or 494)
  • Have lived in a designated regional area for at least three years while holding the provisional visa
  • Have earned a taxable income at or above a specified threshold for at least three years
  • Have complied with all conditions of the provisional visa
  • Meet health and character requirements

Former Subclass 187 (RSMS) Visa

The Regional Sponsored Migration Scheme (RSMS) Subclass 187 visa was closed to new applications on 16 November 2019. It has been replaced by the Subclass 494 visa. If you were granted a Subclass 187 visa, it remains valid, and you hold permanent residency.

If you lodged a Subclass 187 application before the programme closed, your application will continue to be processed under the former requirements. Granger Australia can assist with any outstanding 187 applications or related matters.

Benefits of Regional Migration

Lower Cost of Living

Regional areas typically offer more affordable housing, lower everyday expenses, and a high quality of life compared to major capital cities.

Strong Employment Demand

Many regional employers face genuine skill shortages, making it easier to find sponsorship opportunities and secure long-term employment.

Pathway to Permanent Residency

Regional provisional visas offer a clear pathway to permanent residency through the Subclass 191 visa after meeting the regional living and income requirements.

Priority Processing

Regional visa applications may receive priority processing by the Department of Home Affairs, reflecting the Australian Government's focus on regional growth.

Employer Obligations

Employers who sponsor workers under the Subclass 494 visa have specific obligations, including:

  • Paying the sponsored worker at least the annual market salary rate for the occupation
  • Ensuring the terms and conditions of employment are no less favourable than those provided to equivalent Australian workers
  • Not recovering sponsorship costs from the visa holder
  • Maintaining accurate records and cooperating with monitoring activities
  • Notifying the Department of Home Affairs of any changes in circumstances
Processing Times

Processing times for regional sponsorship visas vary depending on the stream, completeness of the application, and the Department of Home Affairs' workload. For current processing times, visit the Department of Home Affairs website.
