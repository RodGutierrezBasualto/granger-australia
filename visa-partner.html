Partner Visas

The Australian partner visa programme allows the spouse or de facto partner of an Australian citizen, permanent resident or eligible New Zealand citizen to live in Australia permanently. Granger Australia has extensive experience with partner visa applications and can guide you through the two-stage process, from temporary to permanent residency.

The Two-Stage Process

Partner visas are generally processed in two stages. First, a temporary visa is granted, allowing the applicant to live and work in Australia. After a period (usually about two years from the date of application), the permanent visa is assessed. If the relationship is genuine and continuing at that time, the permanent visa is granted.

Exception: If you have been in a long-standing relationship (generally three or more years, or two years if you have a child together), you may be eligible for direct grant of the permanent visa without the two-year waiting period.

Onshore Partner Visa - Subclass 820/801

The onshore partner visa is for applicants who are in Australia at the time of lodging the application. It consists of two components:

Subclass 820 - Temporary

Granted first, allowing the applicant to live and work in Australia while the permanent visa is assessed.

  • Applicant must be in Australia when applying and when the visa is granted
  • Grants full work and study rights
  • Access to Medicare
  • Allows unlimited travel in and out of Australia
Subclass 801 - Permanent

Assessed approximately two years after the date of application for the temporary visa.

  • The relationship must be genuine and continuing at the time of assessment
  • Grants permanent residency in Australia
  • Applicant can travel freely, work without restriction, and access government benefits
  • Pathway to Australian citizenship

Offshore Partner Visa - Subclass 309/100

The offshore partner visa is for applicants who are outside Australia at the time of lodging the application. It also follows the two-stage process:

Subclass 309 - Temporary

Granted first, allowing the applicant to travel to Australia and live with their partner.

  • Applicant must be outside Australia when applying and when the visa is granted
  • Grants full work and study rights in Australia
  • Access to Medicare
  • Valid for approximately two years
Subclass 100 - Permanent

Assessed approximately two years after the date of the temporary visa application.

  • The relationship must be genuine and continuing
  • Grants permanent residency in Australia
  • The applicant can be in or outside Australia when this visa is granted
  • Pathway to Australian citizenship

Prospective Marriage Visa - Subclass 300

The Prospective Marriage visa allows the fiance(e) of an Australian citizen, permanent resident or eligible New Zealand citizen to travel to Australia and marry their partner within nine months. After marriage, the visa holder can then apply for the onshore partner visa (Subclass 820/801).

Key Requirements

  • Both parties must be at least 18 years of age
  • The couple must have met in person (not only online)
  • There must be a genuine intention to marry and live together as partners
  • Both parties must be free to marry (not currently married to someone else)
  • The applicant must be outside Australia when the visa is granted
  • The marriage must take place within nine months of the visa being granted
  • Must meet health and character requirements

Eligible Relationships

Partner visas are available for both married and de facto relationships, including same-sex couples. The Department of Home Affairs recognises:

Married Couples

The marriage must be legally valid under Australian law. Marriages conducted overseas are generally recognised if they were valid under the law of the country where the marriage took place.

De Facto Partners

De facto partners must demonstrate a genuine and continuing relationship for at least 12 months prior to the application, unless there are compelling and compassionate circumstances or the relationship is registered with a state or territory authority.

Same-Sex Couples

Same-sex couples are eligible to apply for partner visas on the same basis as opposite-sex couples, whether married or in a de facto relationship.

Relationship Evidence

The Department of Home Affairs must be satisfied that the relationship is genuine and continuing. Evidence is assessed across four key areas:

Financial Aspects
  • Joint bank accounts or shared financial commitments
  • Joint ownership of property or assets
  • Shared household expenses
Nature of the Household
  • Evidence of living together (lease, utility bills)
  • Shared responsibility for household tasks
  • Joint ownership of household items
Social Aspects
  • Joint social activities and mutual friends
  • Recognition of the relationship by family and friends
  • Photos together over the course of the relationship
Commitment to Each Other
  • Length of the relationship
  • Knowledge of each other's personal details and circumstances
  • Willingness to support each other emotionally and financially
  • Statutory declarations from the couple and third parties

Sponsor Requirements

  • The sponsor must be an Australian citizen, permanent resident or eligible New Zealand citizen
  • The sponsor must be at least 18 years of age
  • A person can generally only sponsor two partner visa applicants in their lifetime
  • There is a minimum five-year waiting period between sponsoring different partners
  • The sponsor must meet character requirements and must not have relevant criminal convictions
  • The sponsor must be approved before the visa can be granted
Processing Times

Processing times for partner visas vary and can be significant. For current processing times, visit the Department of Home Affairs website.
