Australian Visitor Visas

Australia offers several visitor visa options for tourism, business purposes, or visiting family and friends. The right visa depends on your passport, the purpose of your visit, and how long you intend to stay.

Subclass 600 — Visitor Visa

The Visitor visa (Subclass 600) is the main visa for people visiting Australia temporarily. It has several streams:

Tourist Stream
  • For tourism, recreation, or visiting family and friends
  • Stay periods of up to 3, 6, or 12 months depending on circumstances
  • Single or multiple entry options available
  • No work permitted
Business Visitor Stream
  • For attending meetings, conferences, or negotiations
  • Does not permit employment in Australia
  • Stay periods of up to 3 months typically
Sponsored Family Stream
  • For visiting family members in Australia
  • Requires a sponsor who is an Australian citizen, permanent resident, or eligible NZ citizen
  • Sponsor must provide a security bond
  • Stay periods of up to 12 months
General Requirements
  • Valid passport
  • Genuine intention to visit temporarily
  • Adequate funds for your stay
  • Meet health and character requirements
  • Adequate health insurance

Subclass 601 — Electronic Travel Authority (ETA)

A streamlined electronic visa for passport holders from select countries including the USA, Canada, Japan, and South Korea.

  • Stays of up to 3 months at a time
  • Valid for 12 months with multiple entries
  • For tourism or business visitor activities
  • Applied for electronically

Subclass 651 — eVisitor

A free electronic visa available to passport holders from European Union countries and several other European nations.

  • Free of charge
  • Stays of up to 3 months at a time
  • Valid for 12 months with multiple entries
  • Study of up to 3 months permitted

How Granger Australia Can Help

While many visitor visa applications are straightforward, complications can arise with previous visa refusals, health concerns, or complex travel histories. We can assist with:

  • Determining the most appropriate visitor visa for your situation
  • Preparing and lodging your visa application
  • Guidance on supporting documentation
  • Applications involving complicating factors
  • Visa condition compliance and extensions of stay

Note: Processing times vary depending on the stream and individual circumstances. Visit the Department of Home Affairs website for current processing times.
